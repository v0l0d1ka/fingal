Malahide Castle set on 250 acres of park land was both a fortress and a private home for nearly 800 years. In 1185 Henry II of England granted 600 acres of land in Malahide to Richard Talbot. The castle was originally a Norman Tower House until the last quarter of the 18th century when it was extended and castellated.
